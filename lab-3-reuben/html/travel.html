Spirited Away (the bathhouse) Dogo Onsen (Matsuyama, Japan) Dogo Onsen Honkan (one of Japan’s oldest hot springs), the ornate wooden architecture Spring (March-May) or Autumn (September-November) for mild weather Round-trip cost: ~$1,500 – $2,500 CAD This 3,000-year-old onsen is said to have inspired the mystical bathhouse where Chihiro works in Spirited Away.
Howl’s Moving Castle (the colorful, fairy-tale town) Colmar (Alsace, France) Petite Venise (canal district), Maison Pfister, Unterlinden Museum December for Christmas markets or May-June for blooming flowers Edmonton to Paris Round-trip: ~$1,200 – $2,200 CAD
Paris to Colmar by Train: ~$60–$150 CAD		 The half-timbered houses and cobblestone streets of Colmar resemble the magical town where Sophie meets Howl.
Princess Mononoke (the ancient forest) Yakushima (Kagoshima Prefecture, Japan) Shiratani Unsuikyo Ravine, Jomon Sugi (7,000-year-old cedar), Yakusugi Land May-June (mild weather) or September-October (fewer crowds) Edmontonn to Osaka Round-trip: ~$1,400 – $2,400 CAD
Osaka to Kagoshima: ~$120–$200 CAD 		The moss-covered forests of Yakushima directly inspired the mystical woods in Princess Mononoke.
Kiki’s Delivery Service (the charming seaside town) Visby (Gotland, Sweden) Visby City Wall, Gotland Museum, Botanical Gardens Summer (June-August) for warm weather and festivals Edmonton to Stockholm Round-trip: ~$1,400 – $2,400 CAD
Stockholm to Visby One-way: ~$80 – $200 CAD		 The medieval streets and coastal vibe of Visby resemble Koriko, the town where Kiki starts her delivery business.
The Tale of The Princess Kaguya (the serene, natural beauty) Saiho-ji (Kyoto, Japan) – The Moss Temple The lush moss gardens (requires advance reservation) June (rainy season) for vibrant green moss or November for autumn colors Edmonton to Tokyo Round-trip: ~$1,500–$2,500 CAD
Tokyo to Kyoto by Shinkansen (2.5 hrs): ~$150 CAD		 The temple’s tranquil, overgrown gardens mirror the film’s delicate watercolor aesthetic.
